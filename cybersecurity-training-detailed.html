1 Executive Overview

In the critical minerals refining industry, cybersecurity is not just an IT concern—it's a matter of operational continuity, safety, and national security. Syntra Refining's Master Cybersecurity Program establishes a comprehensive framework to protect our industrial control systems, operational technology, and business systems from evolving cyber threats.

Program Mission: To establish and maintain a robust cybersecurity posture that protects Syntra's critical refining operations, safeguards proprietary technology, ensures regulatory compliance, and maintains stakeholder trust while enabling secure operational excellence.

Syntra's cybersecurity program aligns with and exceeds requirements from:

  • NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 - Comprehensive security controls
  • IEC 62443 - Industrial automation and control systems security
  • NERC CIP - Critical Infrastructure Protection standards
  • ISO/IEC 27001 - Information security management
  • CMMC - Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (Level 2+)

3 Critical Asset Protection

3.1 Asset Classification

Syntra categorizes information and system assets into four security tiers:

Tier Asset Type Examples Security Controls
Critical Safety & Operations SCADA systems, Process controls, Safety systems Air-gapped, Multi-factor auth, Continuous monitoring
High Proprietary Tech Refining processes, R&D data, Supply chain systems Encryption, Access controls, Data loss prevention
Medium Business Systems ERP, Financial systems, HR databases Standard encryption, Role-based access, Regular audits
Standard General IT Email, Collaboration tools, Public website Basic security controls, User authentication

3.2 Network Segmentation

Syntra implements a defense-in-depth network architecture with strict segmentation:

  • Level 0 (Process Layer) - Physical sensors and actuators
  • Level 1 (Control Layer) - PLCs, RTUs, and DCS controllers
  • Level 2 (Supervisory Layer) - SCADA and HMI systems
  • Level 3 (Operations Layer) - MES and plant-level operations
  • Level 4 (Enterprise Layer) - ERP and business systems
  • DMZ (Demilitarized Zone) - External-facing services
Critical Control: All traffic between OT (Levels 0-2) and IT (Levels 3-4) must pass through security inspection zones with mandatory deep packet inspection, protocol filtering, and behavioral analysis.

4 Security Operations

4.1 Threat Detection & Monitoring

Syntra operates a 24/7 Security Operations Center (SOC) utilizing:

  • SIEM Platform - Centralized log aggregation and correlation
  • Network Traffic Analysis - Anomaly detection and baseline monitoring
  • Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR) - Host-based threat detection
  • OT Security Monitoring - Industrial protocol analysis and asset tracking
  • Threat Intelligence - Real-time feeds on APTs targeting critical infrastructure

4.2 Incident Response

The incident response process follows these defined phases:

  1. Preparation - Readiness activities, playbook development, team training
  2. Detection & Analysis - Event triage, severity classification, impact assessment
  3. Containment - Immediate isolation, threat neutralization, evidence preservation
  4. Eradication - Root cause removal, system hardening, vulnerability remediation
  5. Recovery - System restoration, validation testing, normal operations resumption
  6. Post-Incident Activity - Lessons learned, documentation, process improvement
Response Time Objectives:
  • Critical incidents: Initial response within 15 minutes
  • High-priority incidents: Initial response within 1 hour
  • Medium-priority incidents: Initial response within 4 hours
  • Low-priority incidents: Initial response within 24 hours

5 Access Control & Identity Management

5.1 Authentication Standards

All Syntra systems implement layered authentication controls:

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) - Required for all remote access and privileged accounts
  • Privileged Access Management (PAM) - Just-in-time access for administrative functions
  • Single Sign-On (SSO) - Centralized identity management for enterprise systems
  • Biometric Authentication - Required for critical facility access and OT system modifications

5.2 Least Privilege Principle

Access rights are granted based on strict need-to-know and need-to-do principles:

  • Default deny posture for all systems and data
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) with regular reviews
  • Time-limited privileged access with approval workflows
  • Automated de-provisioning upon role changes or separation
  • Quarterly access certification by data owners

7 Vendor & Supply Chain Security

7.1 Third-Party Risk Management

All vendors with access to Syntra systems or data must meet security requirements:

  • Pre-engagement Assessment - Security questionnaires, certifications review, financial stability
  • Contract Requirements - Security controls, audit rights, incident notification, insurance
  • Ongoing Monitoring - Quarterly attestations, annual audits, continuous threat intelligence
  • Access Controls - VPN/zero-trust access, activity logging, privileged session recording
  • Incident Response - Joint response procedures, communication protocols, liability terms

7.2 Software & Hardware Supply Chain

Critical systems undergo rigorous supply chain security validation:

  • Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) review for all industrial software
  • Hardware integrity verification and anti-tampering measures
  • Trusted supplier programs with facility audits
  • Secure delivery and chain-of-custody protocols
  • Component authenticity verification

8 Data Protection & Privacy

8.1 Data Classification & Handling

Syntra implements comprehensive data protection controls:

  • Encryption at Rest - AES-256 for all stored data
  • Encryption in Transit - TLS 1.3 for all network communications
  • Data Loss Prevention (DLP) - Automated scanning and blocking of unauthorized transfers
  • Secure Disposal - DoD 5220.22-M standard for data destruction
  • Backup & Recovery - Encrypted, geographically distributed, tested quarterly

8.2 Intellectual Property Protection

Critical Asset: Syntra's proprietary refining processes and critical minerals processing technology represent strategic national assets. Unauthorized disclosure could compromise competitive advantage and national security interests.

Enhanced protection measures for IP include:

  • Need-to-know compartmentalization of sensitive R&D data
  • Digital rights management (DRM) for technical documentation
  • Watermarking and tracking of all proprietary documents
  • Insider threat monitoring program
  • Exit interviews and equipment sanitization for departing employees

9 Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery

9.1 Recovery Objectives

System Category Recovery Time Objective (RTO) Recovery Point Objective (RPO)
Safety Systems < 1 hour Zero data loss
Production Control < 4 hours < 15 minutes
Business Critical < 24 hours < 1 hour
Standard Systems < 72 hours < 24 hours

9.2 Backup Strategy

Syntra implements a comprehensive 3-2-1 backup strategy:

  • 3 copies of critical data (production + 2 backups)
  • 2 different media types (disk + tape/cloud)
  • 1 offsite copy in geographically separated facility

Additional measures include:

  • Immutable backups protected from ransomware encryption
  • Quarterly full restoration tests
  • Air-gapped backup copies for critical OT configurations
  • Automated backup monitoring and alerting
