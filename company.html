Our Journey

Our Story

From identifying a critical infrastructure gap to deploying commercial-scale refining capacity, Syntra Refining is addressing North America's midstream bottleneck in critical minerals processing.

2017-2019: Founding Era

Identifying the Infrastructure Gap

Syntra Refining was founded after founder Nifemi Oguntuase identified a fundamental structural inefficiency in North America's critical minerals value chain. While billions flowed into upstream mining and downstream battery manufacturing, the midstream refining infrastructure required to connect them remained fragmented and offshore.

Through extensive technical research and market analysis, it became clear that the constraint wasn't demand, geology, or capital availability—it was refining capacity. North American manufacturers were dependent on foreign jurisdictions for 86% of battery-grade refined materials, creating supply chain vulnerabilities and cost inefficiencies.

Syntra was established to deploy advanced hydrometallurgical refining infrastructure that could process complex domestic feedstocks at battery-grade purity specifications, operating at commercial scale with economic efficiency.

2020-2022: Development Phase

Process Development & IP Creation

Development phase focused on hydrometallurgical process optimization, pilot-scale validation, and intellectual property development. This period established the technical foundation and proprietary methodologies that differentiate Syntra's approach.

Pilot operations in Halifax, Nova Scotia demonstrated technical feasibility and economic viability of our modular refining approach. Process validation confirmed our systems could handle variable feedstock compositions while maintaining consistent purity output—a critical capability for processing domestic minerals with complex geochemistry.

2023-2024: Infrastructure Development

Building Commercial Infrastructure

Transition from pilot validation to commercial-scale refining infrastructure development in Halifax, Nova Scotia. This phase marks Syntra's evolution from technology developer to infrastructure developer.

The Halifax facility is being designed for initial capacity of 1,000 MT/year, establishing the operational foundation for our modular approach. The facility will process nickel, cobalt, and lithium-bearing feedstocks from North American mining operations, producing battery-grade refined materials that meet automotive OEM specifications.

Development Milestones
  • → Commercial facility design and engineering completed
  • → Offtake agreements secured with major automotive OEMs
  • → Feedstock supply partnerships with mining operations
  • → Site preparation and permitting underway

Our pilot validation confirmed that hydrometallurgical refining at commercial scale is economically competitive with offshore alternatives when supply chain logistics, purity specifications, and strategic security are factored into total cost of ownership.

Since 2025: Infrastructure Expansion

Planning Infrastructure Network

With pilot validation complete and Halifax facility construction underway, Syntra is planning additional modular refining capacity across strategic North American locations, focusing on proximity to mining operations and battery manufacturing hubs.

Our modular infrastructure approach will enable rapid deployment with capital efficiency. Each facility is designed for feedstock flexibility, allowing us to process variable mineral compositions while maintaining consistent battery-grade output specifications.

Expansion Focus Areas

  • → Regional hubs near major mining districts

Strategic Priorities

The objective is clear: establish the refining infrastructure backbone that makes domestic critical minerals supply chains economically competitive and strategically secure. Syntra is building the midstream capacity North America's energy transition requires.
