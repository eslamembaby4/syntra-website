Our Mission

Syntra Refining exists to eliminate the structural bottleneck constraining North America's energy transition. We deploy advanced hydrometallurgical infrastructure that processes complex domestic feedstocks into battery-grade materials at commercial scale.

We are building the critical infrastructure that makes domestic supply chains economically and strategically viable.

Our Vision

Domestically Secured Energy Infrastructure

North America's energy independence requires domestic refining capacity operating at industrial scale. We envision a future where critical minerals are processed on-shore, at battery-grade purity, with the throughput and reliability modern manufacturing demands.

Our Approach

Infrastructure Operators, Not Technology Developers

We are infrastructure operators deploying proven hydrometallurgical systems at commercial scale. Our approach combines established process chemistry with modern automation and AI-driven optimization. The technology exists—the infrastructure doesn't. We build it.

Leadership

Executive Leadership

Building transformative refining infrastructure demands rare convergence: deep technical expertise in process chemistry, institutional-grade resource finance experience, and the strategic vision to recognize market inflections before they become obvious.

Nifemi Oguntuase

Founder & Chief Executive Officer

Process Chemistry Resource Finance Infrastructure Development

Nifemi Oguntuase founded Syntra Refining after identifying a fundamental structural inefficiency in North America's critical minerals value chain. With expertise spanning mineral process chemistry, institutional resource finance, and exploration consulting, she recognized what capital markets had systematically undervalued: while upstream mining and downstream manufacturing attracted billions in investment, the midstream refining bottleneck remained unaddressed.

Her technical background in process chemistry, combined with years advising mining companies and institutional investors on multi-hundred-million-dollar resource development projects, provided unique insight into the market failure. Mining operations had achieved global scale. Battery manufacturers had secured capital. But refining infrastructure—the critical link between raw material and finished product—remained fragmented, offshore, and technically inadequate for processing domestic feedstocks at required purity specifications.

Syntra was founded to solve this problem through deployment of advanced hydrometallurgical infrastructure that replaces energy-intensive pyrometallurgical processes with precision chemical refining. The company's modular approach enables rapid deployment, feedstock flexibility, and battery-grade output specifications that conventional smelting cannot economically achieve.

"North America has the geology, the capital, and the demand. What's missing is the infrastructure to connect them. Syntra exists to build that infrastructure—not as an R&D project, but as operational refining capacity deployed at commercial scale."

Under her leadership, Syntra is establishing the refining infrastructure backbone that makes domestic supply chains economically competitive and strategically secure.
