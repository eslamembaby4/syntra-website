The Problem

North America's energy transition depends on domestic critical minerals supply chains, but over 85% of battery-grade refined materials are processed offshore. The constraint isn't geology or demand—it's refining infrastructure.

Our Solution

Syntra Refining is building the midstream infrastructure to eliminate this bottleneck. We deploy advanced hydrometallurgical refining facilities that process complex domestic feedstocks at battery-grade purity specifications—achieving economic efficiency and strategic security at commercial scale.

OUR STORY

From identifying a critical infrastructure gap to deploying commercial-scale refining capacity, Syntra Refining is addressing North America's midstream bottleneck in critical minerals processing.

2017-2019: Founding Era

IDENTIFYING THE INFRASTRUCTURE GAP

Syntra Refining was founded after founder Nifemi Oguntuase identified a fundamental structural inefficiency in North America's critical minerals value chain. While billions flowed into upstream mining and downstream battery manufacturing, the midstream refining infrastructure required to connect them remained fragmented and offshore.

Through extensive technical research and market analysis, it became clear that the constraint wasn't demand, geology, or capital availability—it was refining capacity. North American manufacturers were dependent on foreign jurisdictions for over 85% of battery-grade refined materials, creating supply chain vulnerabilities and cost inefficiencies.

Syntra was established to deploy advanced hydrometallurgical refining infrastructure that could process complex domestic feedstocks at battery-grade purity specifications, operating at commercial scale with economic efficiency.

2020-2022: Development Phase

PROCESS DEVELOPMENT & IP CREATION

Development phase focused on hydrometallurgical process optimization, pilot-scale validation, and intellectual property development. This period established the technical foundation and proprietary methodologies that differentiate Syntra's approach.

Pilot operations in Halifax, Nova Scotia demonstrated technical feasibility and economic viability of our refining approach. Process validation confirmed our systems could handle variable feedstock compositions while maintaining consistent purity output—a critical capability for processing domestic minerals with complex geochemistry.

2023-2024: Infrastructure Development

BUILDING COMMERCIAL INFRASTRUCTURE

Transition from pilot validation to commercial-scale refining infrastructure development in Halifax, Nova Scotia. This phase marks Syntra's evolution from technology developer to infrastructure developer.

The Halifax facility is being designed for initial capacity of 1,000 TPA (tonnes per annum), establishing the operational foundation for our approach. The facility will process nickel, cobalt, and lithium-bearing feedstocks from North American mining operations, producing battery-grade refined materials that meet automotive OEM specifications.

  • → Feedstock supply partnerships with mining operations

Pilot validation confirmed that hydrometallurgical refining at commercial scale is economically competitive with offshore alternatives when supply chain logistics, purity specifications, and strategic security are factored into total cost of ownership.

Since 2025: Infrastructure Expansion

PLANNING INFRASTRUCTURE NETWORK

With pilot validation complete and Halifax facility construction underway, Syntra is planning additional refining capacity across strategic North American locations, focusing on proximity to mining operations and battery manufacturing hubs.

Our infrastructure approach will enable rapid deployment with capital efficiency. Each facility is designed for feedstock flexibility, allowing us to process variable mineral compositions while maintaining consistent battery-grade output specifications.
