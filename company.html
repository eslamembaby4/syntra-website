OUR STORY

From identifying a critical infrastructure gap to deploying commercial-scale refining capacity, Syntra Refining is addressing North America's midstream bottleneck in critical minerals processing.

2017-2019: Founding Era

IDENTIFYING THE INFRASTRUCTURE GAP

Syntra Refining was founded after founder Nifemi Oguntuase identified a fundamental structural inefficiency in North America's critical minerals value chain. While billions flowed into upstream mining and downstream battery manufacturing, the midstream refining infrastructure required to connect them remained fragmented and offshore.

Through extensive technical research and market analysis, it became clear that the constraint wasn't demand, geology, or capital availability—it was refining capacity. North American manufacturers were dependent on foreign jurisdictions for more than 85% of battery-grade refined materials, creating supply chain vulnerabilities and cost inefficiencies.

Syntra was established to deploy advanced clean hydrometallurgical refining infrastructure that could process complex domestic feedstocks at battery-grade purity specifications, operating at commercial scale with economic efficiency.

2023-2024: Infrastructure Development

BUILDING CAPACITY

Facility scale refining infrastructure development in Halifax, Nova Scotia. This phase marks Syntra's evolution from technology developer to infrastructure developer.

Pilot validation confirmed that our hydrometallurgical refining at commercial scale is economically competitive with offshore alternatives when supply chain logistics, purity specifications, and strategic security are factored into total cost of ownership.

The Halifax facility is being designed for initial capacity of 1,000 TPA (tonnes per annum), establishing the operational foundation for our approach. The facility will process nickel, cobalt, and lithium-bearing feedstocks from North American mining operations, producing battery-grade refined materials that meet automotive OEM specifications.

SINCE 2025 | INFRASTRUCTURE EXPANSION

PLANNING INFRASTRUCTURE NETWORK

With pilot validation complete and construction of SYNHUBc Halifax underway, Syntra is planning additional modular refining capacity across strategic North American locations, focusing on proximity to mining operations and battery manufacturing hubs.

Our modular infrastructure approach will enable rapid deployment with capital efficiency. Each facility is designed for feedstock flexibility, allowing us to process variable mineral compositions while maintaining consistent battery-grade output specifications.
